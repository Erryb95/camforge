The gap

“I have built a fully functional pipe cutting machine… The problem is SheetCAM. It generates the toolpath, but the post processor is not compatible with QtPlasmaC.” — LinuxCNC forum, “No CAM option for Rotary QtPlasmac?!”

People building rotary tube machines end up hand-editing G0 A90 between cuts. Commercial CAM either can't post to QtPlasmaC or costs 3–4× more and still skips native rotary. LGE is built for exactly this corner.

How it works

Flat pattern in, QtPlasmaC out.

Three steps, all in the browser — no install, no cloud, your files stay on your machine.

01 — UNROLL

Draw on the developed tube

Import a DXF (or STEP profile), or lay out holes and slots on the tube's flat pattern. u = along the tube, v = around it.

02 — WRAP

Wrap onto the A axis

The design maps to the rotary angle (A = the true section angle) with kerf compensation, lead-in/out and inside-out sequencing.

03 — POST

Export & simulate

Get QtPlasmaC G-code (G93 inverse-time, tube-cut, torch-follow Z) plus a matching material file. Watch it cut in 3D first.

What's inside

A real plasma CAM, not a viewer with a toy button.

⌀

Round & rectangular tube

True geometric A-axis mapping. Torch-follow Z keeps standoff on rectangular sections where the radius changes.

↹

Kerf compensation

Outer +kerf/2, holes −kerf/2, Hypertherm swirl direction (outer CW, holes CCW). Split holes are handled, tiny ones flagged.

⟿

Lead-in / lead-out

Tangent arc lead-ins from the scrap side, hole overburn (QtPlasmaC #<oclength>), inside-out ordering.

▦

Real cut charts

Mild steel, stainless 304, aluminium, FineCut, F5 — kerf / feed / pierce / arc-volts from Hypertherm Powermax SYNC charts.

P

QtPlasmaC material files

Export a ready *_material.cfg and select it in the program with M190 P<n>. No hand-typing cut charts.

▶

Simulate before you burn

3D tube, developed view, code↔part sync. See every cut wrap around the tube before it touches metal.
